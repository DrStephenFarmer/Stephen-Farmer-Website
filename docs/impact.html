Impact & Funding

This research programme is designed to generate impact throughout its development rather than only upon completion.

Each stage produces both scientific knowledge and practical outputs, creating opportunities to inform professional practice, training, policy, and public understanding while progressively contributing towards the long-term goal of estimating eyewitness reliability.

Impact Philosophy

Eyewitness evidence remains one of the most influential forms of evidence presented in criminal investigations and criminal trials. Yet decisions regarding eyewitness reliability are often made under conditions of considerable uncertainty.

The overarching aim of this programme is to develop scientifically grounded methods for estimating eyewitness reliability and translating those findings into practical resources capable of supporting evidence-based decision-making within the justice system.

Rather than viewing impact as the final outcome of a completed research programme, this approach treats impact as a cumulative process generated throughout successive stages of research.

Impact Across the Programme

Stage 1: Witnessing Conditions

Research

  • Retention interval
  • Separation distance
  • Exposure duration

Potential Deliverables

  • Police training materials
  • Professional briefings
  • Expert witness evidence base
  • Public-facing resources on eyewitness memory

Potential Impact

Improved understanding of how witnessing conditions influence eyewitness reliability.

Stage 2: Decision Indicators

Research

  • Confidence
  • Response time
  • Confidence–accuracy relationships

Potential Deliverables

  • Confidence interpretation guidance
  • Police interview guidance
  • Judicial training resources
  • Expert witness materials

Potential Impact

Improved interpretation of witness confidence and decision behaviour.

Stage 3: Evidence Accumulation and Decision Processes

Research

  • Evidence accumulation
  • Computational decision modelling
  • Evidence diagnosticity

Potential Deliverables

  • Mechanistic explanations of eyewitness decisions
  • Open-source modelling resources
  • Computational research infrastructure

Potential Impact

Improved scientific understanding of eyewitness decision-making and enhanced predictive modelling.

Stage 4: Ecological Validation

Research

  • Realistic witnessing environments

Potential Deliverables

  • Law-enforcement training environments
  • Collaborative research platforms
  • Public engagement demonstrations

Potential Impact

Greater confidence that research findings generalise to realistic witnessing situations.

Stage 5: Predictive Reliability Estimation

Research

Potential Deliverables

  • Practitioner guidance
  • Investigative decision-support resources

Potential Impact

Development of transparent and empirically grounded methods for estimating eyewitness reliability.

Stage 6: Translation to Justice

Research

  • Probability communication
  • Policy translation

Potential Deliverables

  • Jury education resources
  • Judicial guidance
  • Policy recommendations
  • Decision-support frameworks

Potential Impact

Improved interpretation and evaluation of eyewitness evidence within legal settings.

Professional and Policy Relevance

The programme has direct relevance to:

  • Eyewitness identification procedures
  • Police investigations
  • Judicial guidance on identification evidence
  • Juror interpretation of eyewitness testimony
  • Communication of uncertainty in criminal trials
  • Evidence-based reform of legal safeguards
  • Criminal justice policy

The long-term objective is to contribute to guidance that is psychologically informed, empirically grounded, and practically usable within real-world legal settings.

Funding Opportunities

The programme creates opportunities for funding across multiple research domains, including:

  • Cognitive psychology and memory
  • Legal and forensic psychology
  • Criminal justice research
  • Computational social science
  • Artificial intelligence and decision-support systems
  • Virtual and augmented reality
  • Public policy and evidence communication

Potential funders include:

  • ESRC
  • UK Research and Innovation
  • Leverhulme Trust
  • Nuffield Foundation
  • College of Policing
  • Ministry of Justice
  • Innovate UK

The programme structure allows multiple independent funding streams while maintaining a coherent long-term vision.

Long-Term Vision

The long-term ambition of the programme is to develop scientifically grounded, transparent, and explainable methods for estimating the probability that an eyewitness identification is correct under specified conditions.

By integrating memory science, decision science, computational modelling, metacognition, and immersive technologies, the programme aims to support more informed evaluation of eyewitness evidence and contribute to fairer, more accurate, and more transparent justice outcomes.
